## Working title

**Foxhound Design Partner Program**

---

## Core pitch

Foxhound helps teams turn production AI agent failures into safer future releases.

We’re looking for a small number of engineering-led teams running AI agents or copilots in production who want help with:
- understanding regressions faster
- replaying and diffing bad runs
- turning real failures into eval inputs
- validating fixes before wider rollout
- monitoring cost and reliability impact

---

## Best-fit design partners

We are looking for teams that:
- already have AI agents or copilots in production
- have experienced debugging pain, regressions, or release-confidence issues
- can share real workflow context, even if they cannot share sensitive data broadly
- are willing to give candid product feedback

---

## What partners get

- direct founder access
- hands-on workflow walkthroughs
- fast feedback loop on onboarding and product rough edges
- influence on product direction for the production improvement loop
- early access to emerging workflows and GTM narrative shaping

---

## What Foxhound focuses on in the pilot

The core workflow we want to prove is:
1. identify a bad or degraded production run
2. inspect trace context
3. replay and diff behavior
4. turn weak runs into evaluation material
5. validate a fix or candidate change
6. understand cost / SLA / regression implications before promotion

---

## What we ask from partners

- 2–4 short working sessions over the pilot period
- blunt product feedback
- examples of where current tooling breaks down
- willingness to tell us where the workflow does not yet earn its place

Optional if things go well:
- short testimonial or quote
- permission to share a redacted workflow story
- longer-term pilot / paid design-partner discussion

---

## Suggested pilot structure

### Week 1
- kickoff conversation
- understand current AI workflow and failure modes
- align on one concrete workflow to test

### Week 2
- run Foxhound against a representative scenario or demo analogue
- identify friction and missing pieces

### Week 3
- validate whether the loop is genuinely better than current workflow
- collect strongest objections and strongest moments of value

### Week 4
- assess whether the product is worth continued use / deeper pilot
- capture proof points and follow-up path

---

## Success criteria

A design-partner pilot is successful if at least one of these becomes true:
- the team wants to continue using Foxhound
- the team says the loop solves a real painful problem
- the team identifies a very clear adoption blocker worth fixing
- the team is willing to recommend or discuss the product further

---

## Low-friction ask version

I’m looking for a few teams willing to pressure-test a product I’m building for debugging and improving production AI agent behavior. The main workflow is replaying bad runs, diffing behavior, deriving evals from failures, and validating fixes before rollout. If that sounds relevant, I’d love to do a lightweight 20–30 minute review and get brutally honest feedback.
